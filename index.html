About Me

Hello, I'm
Islam Bouikiri

I'm a photography student with a deep curiosity for the world around me. My camera is how I slow down — how I notice what others walk past and find the story hidden in the everyday. Through this course, photography became more than a skill; it became a way of seeing.

Artist Statement

"I like to think of my photographs as frozen memories."

01

I like to think of my photographs as frozen memories. I am drawn to capturing ordinary moments, the things we pass by every day without noticing. What motivates me is the idea that there is a unique beauty in everything we encounter on a daily basis, if we simply pause and look closer. Sometimes, when I show my photos to others, they ask where I found such scenes, and I point to places they visit all the time but never stop to admire. Photography, for me, began as a way to slow down and appreciate my surroundings, and over time, it has become a way to reflect more deeply on life and recognize the beauty in what already exists around us.

02

When I take photos, I try to stay as real as possible. I don't like forcing moments or asking people to act a certain way just to get a "good" picture. I enjoy capturing things as they are — especially when photographing people during events. The best moments, for me, are the spontaneous ones: laughter, nervousness, happiness, feelings that people can look back on and truly recognize. I think those are the images that hold the most meaning. In general, I'm most drawn to simple, everyday scenes that might seem ordinary but can make you stop and reflect.

03

Right now, I'm exploring photography as a way of telling stories, not just through single images but through a series of them. Working on projects where I had to connect different photos helped me see how moments can come together to create a bigger meaning. It pushed me to think more intentionally about what I capture and why. I want to keep developing this, challenging myself to go beyond individual shots and build something more connected. In the end, I just want my work to reveal meaning in the unnoticed and to encourage people to see the world with greater attention and appreciation.
